From signup to closed deals in four visual stages

The flow is simple: deploy your assistant, connect a local number, feed it your product and playbook, then plug it into your CRM or automation. Under the hood, Dealvox runs a custom training loop that keeps improving call quality with every conversation.

Stage 1 · Choose persona, voice, and subscription

Create your Dealvox account and spin up an assistant in a few clicks. Pick one of the five psychology-based personas, then choose a voice that matches your market: accent, gender, age, and speaking style. Decide whether you prefer a subscription minute package or pay-as-you-go calls and you’re ready to move to deployment.

Stage 2 · Connect a real phone number

Purchase a clean, local phone number directly in the interface. The number auto-links to your assistant and becomes its caller ID. You can maintain multiple numbers across markets, test separate campaigns, or segment by product line without touching telecom configs manually.

Stage 3 · Train with your products and outcomes

Add an introductory prompt, define the assistant’s behaviour, and upload product descriptions, features, benefits, and FAQs. Set the desired outcome: send a payment link, book a meeting, or warm-transfer to a human agent. If you don’t have scripts ready, your personal Dealvox chat assistant helps you draft prompts, talk tracks, and offer summaries on the spot.

Stage 4 · Automate calls from your CRM and close the loop

Dealvox exposes a secure endpoint where your CRM, ERP, or automation tool (n8n, Make, Zapier, in-house APIs) can send call requests. A second webhook returns structured results for every call: outcome, notes, intent, and next steps. The built-in training loop uses this data to refine ice-breakers, discovery questions, objection handling, and closing behaviour over time.

Pick the sales persona that matches your buyers

Start by renting one of five psychology-backed voice agents. Each persona is tuned for a different way of making decisions – from highly analytical buyers to emotionally driven ones.

Persona #1
The Challenger
Educates, reframes, and challenges assumptions to unlock new demand.
  • Best for: B2B, SaaS, logistics, technical sales.
  • Style: Analytical, assertive, counter-evidence driven.
  • Watch out: Can feel dominant if empathy is weak.
Persona #2
The Empathizer
Builds trust and comfort in emotionally loaded decisions.
  • Best for: Real estate, healthcare, hospitality, advisory roles.
  • Style: Consultative, patient, deep listening.
  • Watch out: Risks over-accommodating or delaying the close.
Persona #3
The Driver
Pushes momentum with sharp, outcome-focused communication.
  • Best for: High-volume B2C, finance, automotive, outbound teams.
  • Style: Clear next steps, confident calls-to-action.
  • Watch out: May overlook nuance or feel impatient.
Persona #4
The Analyst
Wins credibility with logic, data, and provable ROI.
  • Best for: Tech, engineering, pharma, complex solutions.
  • Style: Structured, precise, documentation-backed.
  • Watch out: Can feel “cold” without human warmth.
Persona #5
The Storyteller
Converts through narrative, emotion, and vivid future states.
  • Best for: Luxury, media, lifestyle, branding, fundraising.
  • Style: Charismatic, visual, aspirational.
  • Watch out: Needs structure to avoid over-promising.
Inside one Dealvox call

What your assistant actually does on the line

Every conversation follows a structured, psychology-aware path — from the first hello to the handoff. You define the goal; the assistant handles the heavy lifting.

Step 1

Break the ice and qualify

The assistant opens with a natural ice-breaker, introduces the offer, and quickly checks if the prospect matches your target profile. It mirrors pace and tone, reducing resistance in the first seconds of the call.

Step 2

Map needs and handle objections

Using the product knowledge you upload, the agent asks focused questions, surfaces pain points, and responds to common objections. It connects benefits to what the prospect actually cares about, instead of reading a static script.

Step 3

Close, schedule, or hand off

Based on the outcome you choose, the assistant sends a link, books a meeting, or routes the call to a live rep once the prospect is “warmed”. Every result is pushed back to your system through the results endpoint so the next steps are fully automated.
